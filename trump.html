Donald J. Trump - Profile

Introduction

Donald John Trump is the 45th President of the United States, serving from January 20, 2017, to January 20, 2021. Before entering politics, Trump was a successful businessman and television personality. He became a prominent figure in American politics as a Republican candidate in the 2016 presidential election.

Trump's presidency was marked by his policies on tax cuts, deregulation, trade, and immigration, as well as his handling of the COVID-19 pandemic and foreign relations.

Early Life and Career

Donald Trump was born on June 14, 1946, in Queens, New York City, to Fred and Mary Trump. He attended the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania, where he earned a degree in economics. He later took over his father's real estate business, which he renamed The Trump Organization.

Trump expanded the company into a global brand, creating skyscrapers, casinos, and resorts. He became a household name as the host of the reality television show "The Apprentice," which ran from 2004 to 2015.

Key Achievements

  • Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017, which lowered corporate tax rates and provided tax cuts to individuals.
  • Successfully negotiated the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA), replacing NAFTA.
  • Appointed three Supreme Court Justices, shaping the Court's conservative direction for years to come.
  • Promoted "America First" foreign policies, including withdrawing from the Paris Agreement on climate change and renegotiating international trade deals.
  • Signed legislation to reform criminal justice with the First Step Act.
